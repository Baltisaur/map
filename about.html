About me

I'm a 22 year old Ruby and Rails developer and university student living in Brisbane, Australia. I enjoy riding bicycles, motorbikes, taking photos and travelling.

My love of computers started when the old man showed me Prince of Persia on a 386. I bounced from a gamer interested in PC hardware, to a Linux fanatic who would install a distribution on anything with 1's and 0's before developing a genuine passion for software.

Since February 2008 I've been employed part-time as a Ruby on Rails developer for Everyday Hero, a service for empowering not for profits with the tools to multiply their fundraising efforts.

I'm studying a Bachelor of Information Technology full-time at Griffith University where I'm in my second year and loving it.

You can follow me on Twitter, hear my musical taste on Last.fm or browse my photos on Flickr. I'm also on Facebook, but I don't visit very often.

Feel free to drop me an e-mail, tate@tatey.com

About this site

It's generated by Jekyll, a blog-aware, static site generator in Ruby. Posts are in Markdown and the source resides on GitHub, never to be lost again. Site conforms to the XHTML 1.0 and CSS 2.1 specifications. All pages are valid and semantic. If you find an error, please let me know.

Seven iterations since 2003

I purchased tatey.com in 2003 and kicked it off with MovableType. In 2004, I switched to WordPress and introduced the lime and green colour that has been in each subsequent iteration. Two iterations over 2004 and 2005, tatey.com remained unchanged until 2008.

Inspired by Pascal Klein's love of the Georgia typeface, I set out to create a Ruby on Rails based blog. Today, you're browsing the seventh iteration.
